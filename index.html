P8105 Final Project: High-Cost Patients in MEPS 2023

P8105 Final Project · Columbia Public Health
High-Cost Patients in U.S. Healthcare: Evidence from MEPS 2023
Using the 2023 Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS), we replicate and extend Mitchell (2023) to study how healthcare spending is concentrated, who the high-cost patients are, which conditions drive spending, and how costs are distributed across services and payers.

Project overview

Key findings at a glance
We summarize three headline insights from our analysis of 2023 MEPS high-cost patients.
Concentration
Spending is concentrated in high-cost groups
Expenditures are extremely concentrated: a small group of “hot spotters” accounts for a disproportionate share of total costs.
Who are they?
Older & multi-morbid
High-cost patients are disproportionately adults aged 45+ with multiple CCSR conditions across cardiovascular, endocrine, and mental health domains.
Prediction
Simple models go a long way
Age and a simple multimorbidity count explain most of the predictive signal; adding many diagnosis indicators only modestly improves accuracy.
Study workflow
  • 1
    Import and clean 2023 MEPS person-level (HC-251) and condition-level (HC-249) files, harmonizing missing values and survey design variables.
  • 2
    Define expenditure groups (Bottom 50%, 50–90%, Top 10%, Top 5%, Top 1%) using survey-weighted quantiles of total expenditures (TOTEXP23).
  • 3
    Describe demographics, income/poverty, insurance, and clinical conditions across cost groups, and fit regression models to predict high-cost status.
  • 4
    Build an interactive Shiny dashboard to allow users to explore expenditure patterns by age, sex, race/ethnicity, income, and insurance coverage.
High-cost patients
We define high-cost expenditure groups (Top 1%, 5%, 10%, Bottom 50%) using survey-weighted percentiles of total annual expenditures (TOTEXP23) under the MEPS design. We then compare age, sex, race/ethnicity, income, and insurance coverage across these groups to characterize who appears in each segment.
Conditions & disease burden
Using the 2023 Medical Conditions file (HC-249) and CCSR codes, we summarize diagnoses at the person level and construct a simple multimorbidity count. We then examine how key condition groups and disease burden vary across expenditure groups, focusing on high-cost patients.
Services, payers & dashboards
We decompose annual spending by service type (inpatient, outpatient, ER, prescriptions, home health) and by payer (out-of-pocket, private insurance, Medicare, Medicaid) to highlight where money is being spent. These summaries feed into an interactive shiny dashboard that allows users to explore expenditure patterns by demographic and insurance profiles.
