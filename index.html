P8105 Final Project: High-Cost Patients in MEPS 2023

P8105 Final Project · Columbia Public Health
High-Cost Patients in U.S. Healthcare: Evidence from MEPS 2023
Using the 2023 Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS), we replicate and extend Mitchell (2023) to study how healthcare spending is concentrated, who the high-cost patients are, which conditions drive spending, and how costs are distributed across services and payers.
Explore code & results Read project proposal

Overview

Below is a quick overview of our project. For details, please use the navigation bar or the buttons above.

High-cost patients
We define expenditure groups (top 1%, 5%, 10%, bottom 50%) using survey-weighted percentiles of total annual expenditures (TOTEXP23). We then describe age, sex, race/ethnicity, income, and insurance across these groups.
Conditions & disease burden
Using the 2023 Medical Conditions file (HC-249) and CCSR categories, we identify the most commonly treated conditions and quantify their contribution to total spending among the top 5% of spenders.
Services, payers & dashboards
We decompose expenditures by service type (inpatient, outpatient, ER, prescriptions, home health) and payer (out-of-pocket, private insurance, Medicare, Medicaid), and implement interactive visualizations using plotly and flexdashboard.
